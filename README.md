## About This Project

This repository contains the research report, presentation slides, and survey questionnaire for a field survey study conducted on children's mental well-being in urban Pabna, Bangladesh.

The study explores how family environment, family structure, screen exposure, and selected socio-demographic factors are associated with the mental well-being of typically developing children and children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

## Project Overview

Children’s mental well-being is influenced by the environment in which they grow up. This study examines whether children’s mental well-being is shaped more by the type of family they live in or by the quality of emotional support, safety, communication, and learning encouragement they receive at home.

The research focuses on children aged **6–12 years** and compares two groups:
* **Typically Developing** children
* **Children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)**

## Research Objectives

<details open>

<br>

To understand how family environment and family structure are connected with children’s mental well-being among typically developing and ASD children in urban Pabna.

### 1. Family Background
* Explore the home environment in which children grow up.
* Identify the major family structures, including nuclear, joint, and single-parent families.

### 2. Child Mental Well-Being
* Assess children’s mental well-being based on parent-reported information.
* Compare the mental well-being of typically developing children and children with ASD.

### 3. Relationship Analysis
* Examine whether a supportive family environment is linked with better mental well-being.
* Compare children’s mental well-being across different family structures.
* Observe how family support, screen exposure, and selected background factors relate to children’s well-being.

## Key Findings

* **Family environment** showed a positive relationship with children’s mental well-being.
* **Parent-child quality time** and **emotional safety** were among the strongest family-related factors.
* **Typically developing children** showed higher mental well-being scores than children with ASD.
* **Family structure alone** did not strongly explain children’s mental well-being after considering other factors.
* **Balanced and guided screen use** may be important for children's mental and behavioral development.
* **Mother’s education** appeared to be an important factor associated with children’s mental well-being.

> **💡 Core Dynamic** > Overall, the quality of care, emotional safety, and support inside the family appeared more important than family type alone.

### Main Conclusion
The study suggests that children's mental well-being is shaped more by **how the family functions** than by the family structure itself. 

A caring, calm, emotionally safe, and learning-friendly home environment may play an important role in improving children's mental well-being, especially among children with ASD.
